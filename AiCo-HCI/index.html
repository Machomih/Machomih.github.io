Introduction

AiCo application is dedicated to air soft enjoyers who who want to have an easier experience on the field and improve teamwork. The application would provide capabilities to make reservations, divide into teams, enter voice chats, track your performance, track your ammunition, improve the orientation through a compass displayed on main screen and possibility to send SOS signals to teammates.

The application is available on mobile phone with full features and on smartwatch devices with limited features that include: compass, ammo track, sos signal and kill/death signal.

my high-level goals are to:

  • To provide a easy and enjoyable experience on the field.
  • Be fully functioning on smartphones and smartwatches.
  • Easier communication between teammates.
  • To improve orientation on battlefield.
  • User-friendly interface.

My scope is to digitalize the experience of a battlefield and help the consumers with easing the need to remember details about the game. Also, I aim to provide an easy way of reserving, matchmaking and calculating score for air soft business owners so that they can spend less time on the digital part of the business and more on the customer interaction and experience.

Detailed Description:

George Doe is a student at Computer Science Faculty of UAIC. Due to spending too much time in front of the screen, he loves to do outdoor activities like hiking, swimming and paintball. Recently he tried reserved alongside his friends a match of air soft. Due to recommendation of the staff there, he installed the AiCo app and figured immediately how everything works. He was pleased to see that the app shows a preview of the filed and more details about the main missions of the game. Also, when he entered on the voice channel his team created, he felt like in a real life battlefield.

George is also happy of the quick process for reserving another match and the effortlessly planning needed for it.

Scenarios:

George uses the app alongside his teammates and gets assigned to a team. He analyze before the game details about the field he is playing on and missions assigned to the game. He enters details about the ammo got and checks the signal send on smartwatch app for when the game starts. After many kills and deaths, he sees that the time for current session is gone. Back to the waiting rom, he checks the score in the app and finds out that being good at CS:GO is not the same as being good at a real-life situation but determined to do a comeback he and his friends reserve another game next week.

Story-boards

Scenario 1

In this scenario I George, my main character that is using the AiCo app on mobile phone.

In the first picture he is using the app to make a reservation for him and his friends.

In the next photo they are at the field and through the app, they are dividing in 2 teams.

Next they check their timer on the app to know when the game starts.

Finally, once the game is over, they check the leaderboard on the app.

There is no usability of the mobile phone on field due to safety of the phone and owner wallet.

Scenario 2

In this scenario I have George using AiCo app on smartwatch.

In the first picture, George is informing a teammate about the position of an enemy but not knowing his orientation, the teammates uses the compass on the app to locate the enemy.

In the next photo, George is using the app on smartwatch to check on his ammo left.

Next he is flanked by enemies and is sending an SOS signal through the app for his teammates to help him.

In the last part, his teammate comes late to help him and sees George dead. Then George remembers to set himself dead through the app so that the final score will adjust accordingly.
